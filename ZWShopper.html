Zero-waste Shopper

This versatile bag bridges the worlds of zero-waste sewing and handweaving to create a functional and sustainable object.

Not for Sale

The weave structure of this project is an interpretation of the classic overshot design, Whig Rose. This traditional pattern, renowned for its intricate and elegant motifs, has been reimagined with a modern block motif in the centre.

By combining a wool tabby with a cotton pattern weft, the design gains rich texture and dimension. After washing and pressing, the wool shrinks, bringing the smooth cotton to the forefront against the matte wool background.

This project features the #zwshopper pattern designed by Bag.uettes. The pattern maximizes fabric usage, eliminating waste while creating a functional, wearable object.

Equipment

  • 4-shaft loom
  • 10-dent reed
  • 2 shuttles
  • Darning needle
  • Hand sewing supplies

Yarn

  • Warp: 2/16 worsted wool — 4,400 yds/lb
  • Tabby weft: 2/16 worsted wool — 4,400 yds/lb
  • Supplemental weft: 2/8 unmercerized cotton — 3,360 yds/lb

Specifications

  • Sett: 20 EPI in 10-dent reed, sleyed 2 ends per dent
  • Ends: 340
  • Width in reed: 17" / 43.2 cm
  • On-loom dimensions: 15.3" W × 26" L
  • Finished dimensions: 14" W × 22.5" L
  • Bag dimensions: 10" L × 6" W × 2" D
  • Warp length: 1.7 yards, including 30" for loom loss and 8" for fringe
  • Finishing: warm wash to full, spin dry, hard press
