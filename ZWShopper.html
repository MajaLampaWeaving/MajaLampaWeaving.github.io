Zero-waste Shopper

This versatile bag bridges the worlds of zero-waste sewing and handweaving to create a functional and sustainable object.

Not for Sale
  • Buy Pattern
  • Read About the Process

The weave structure of this project is an interpretation of the classic overshot design, Whig Rose. This traditional pattern, renowned for its intricate and elegant motifs, has been reimagined with a modern, block motif in the center.
By combining a wool tabby with a cotton pattern weft, the design gains rich texture and dimension. After washing and pressing, the wool shrinks, bringing the smooth cotton to the forefront against the matte wool background.
This interplay creates a striking visual effect, highlighting the intricate motifs of the adapted Whig Rose pattern.

This project features the #zwshopper pattern designed by the innovative brand Bag.uettes. Known for their commitment to sustainability, Bag.uettes creates patterns that maximize fabric usage, eliminating waste. This thoughtful approach reduces environmental impact and encourages creativity in design. Each piece is carefully planned to ensure every bit of fabric is utilized, resulting in stylish and eco-friendly creations that are both functional and fashionable.

Equipment

  • 4-shaft loom
  • 10-dent reed
  • 2 shuttles
  • Darning needle
  • Hand sewing supplies

Yarn

  • Warp: 2/16 worsted wool (4,400 yds/lb)
  • Tabby Weft: 2/16 worsted wool (4,400 yds/lb)
  • Supplemental Weft: 2/8 unmercerized cotton (3,360 yds/lb)

Specifications

  • Sett: 20 EPI in 10-dent reed (2 ends per dent)
  • Ends: 340
  • Width in reed: 17" (43.2 cm)
  • On-loom dimensions: 15.3" W x 26" L
  • Finished dimensions: 14" W x 22.5" L
  • Bag dimensions: 10" L x 6" W x 2" D
  • Finishing: Hand or machine wash in warm, soapy water to full. Spin dry until most moisture is removed. Hard press.
  • Warp length: 1.7 yards, including 30" for loom loss and 8" for fringe.
